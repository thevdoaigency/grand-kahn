01 / The Shot Guide

Every Shot

Each shot has a picture. Copy the picture. Orange tag = modular (works in every future GK video). Blue tag = this job only. If you must choose, get the orange ones.

F. Foundation — Every Site Modular

The eight core frames. Get these on every jobsite. Spine of every video.

F.1 / THE WORKER
35mm · EYE LEVEL

The Worker

Chest up. Focus on face or hands. Do not pose them — let them work.

Camera at their eye level. Never above.

F.2 / THE SCALE
24mm · WIDE

The Scale

Pull way back. Put 1-2 tiny people in frame. The building wins.

People small. Structure big.

F.3 / POWER ANGLE
20mm · LOW

Power Angle

Camera near the ground. Point up at a beam, crane, or worker on a ladder. Sky behind.

Use once or twice. Not for every shot.

F.4 / THE DETAIL
50mm · MACRO

The Detail

Very close. Hands. Tools. Wires. Texture.

Longest lens you have. Or move in.

F.5 / THE LIGHT
35mm · BACKLIT

The Light

Find the sun. Silhouette workers against the light. Chase dust and sparks.

Golden hour best. Any strong light works.

F.6 / THE MOTION
35mm · 60fps

The Motion

Anything moving. Pan with it, or hold steady and let it move through frame.

Shoot at 60fps.

F.7 / THE WIDE
16mm · ESTABLISHING

The Wide

The whole site in one frame. Get high — top floor, hill, parking structure. Drone if cleared.

Hold 8-10 seconds.

F.8 / THE SKY FRAME
35mm · NEGATIVE SPACE

The Sky Frame

Crane or building against big open sky. Subject on grid line, not center.

Titles will land in the sky. Leave space.

A. Brand & Identity Modular

These are gold. They anchor every future Grand Kahn piece. Chase them hard early in the day while the crew is fresh and the trucks are still parked clean.

A.1 / THE MARK
50mm · CLOSE

The Mark

Tight on the GK logo. Hard hat, back of vest, uniform patch, tool case sticker. Anywhere the brand sits on a working person or working thing.

Longest lens you have. Let the person do their job — they don't need to hold still.

A.2 / THE TRUCK
24mm · WIDE

The Truck

GK truck arriving, parked, tailgate open with tools. The brand on wheels is a story beat we'll reuse in every video.

Best if you catch this at arrival, before the truck gets dusty. Try both a wide and a slow push toward the logo.

B. The Install Specific

The finished work. Whichever site you're on, get in close on the electrical craft — this is what nobody else in the pipeline will have.

B.1 / SWITCHGEAR
35mm · LOW ANGLE

The Switchgear

The big metal cabinets. Hero shot — low angle, clean composition. Put a person at the edge for scale if you can.

Ask the foreman which cabinet is the main service. That one is the money shot.

B.2 / CONDUIT RUN
35mm · LEADING LINE

The Conduit Run

Parallel pipes marching away from camera. Repeating geometry = precision = craft. Find the cleanest run on the site and shoot it as a leading line.

The bends are where the skill shows. If a run has a nice 90-degree turn, get that in close too.

B.3 / THE CANOPY
20mm · UP

The Canopy

Solar canopy shot from underneath. Repeating panel pattern overhead, columns dropping into frame. If there's a bus at the charger, that's the whole story in one shot.

Canopy site only. Skip if you're at the Tesla plant.

B.4 / PANEL OPEN
50mm · CLOSE

Panel Open

If the foreman will open a junction box or panel, get in close. Wire bundles, color coding, terminal blocks — this is the craft made visible.

Ask before you shoot inside anything. Some panels are live even after commissioning.

C. Walking with the Foreman Modular

The foreman narrating the job is our narration bed. Even when he's just talking to you off-camera, the body-worn mic should be rolling. Half of what he says will become voiceover.

C.1 / THE FOLLOW
35mm · TRACKING

The Follow

Foreman walking the site, camera behind him at hip height. Handheld is fine. Let the site pass around him.

Keep him talking. Even if you don't use the visual, his voice while walking is pure gold as VO.

C.2 / THE POINT
50mm · OVER SHOULDER

The Point

Foreman's hand pointing at what he built. Shoot from behind his shoulder so both his gesture and the install are in frame.

Ask him to walk you through a specific piece and pause where he'd naturally gesture. Don't direct the point — let it happen.

D. Craft Symbols Modular

Small, specific, timeless. Every one of these will get pulled into future edits. Shoot each of these as a close macro if you have the lens.

D.1 / HANDS ON TOOL
50mm · MACRO

Hands on Tool

Meter, tester, torque driver, wire strippers — whatever they're actually using. Close on the tool in the hand.

Ask them to do the action twice. First take is real, second take you shoot for the camera.

D.2 / BOOTS AND BELT
35mm · WAIST DOWN

Boots and Belt

Tool belt with the radio clipped on. Boots on grating or concrete. The uniform of the trade.

Look for boots in motion — a step onto a ladder rung, or a foot planted next to conduit.

02 / The Ask

Interview Questions

Blueprint-blue blocks are for this job. Orange blocks are evergreen — the answers he gives here become quotes for every Grand Kahn video for the next two years. Push those a little harder.

A · Warmup / Get Him Comfortable
Warmup
  1. What's your name, your role, and how long have you been at Grand Kahn?
  2. How'd you get into electrical work?
B · This Job — Specific
The Install
  1. Where are we, and what did this facility need from Grand Kahn?
  2. Walk me through what Grand Kahn built here.
  3. What was the hardest part of this one?
  4. What's something someone looking at the finished job wouldn't realize?
  5. How long did it take, and how big was your crew?
C · Craft — Evergreen (This is the Gold)
The Craft
  1. What does a great electrical install actually look like? How can you tell one from a bad one?
  2. What separates a good electrician from a great one?
  3. What separates Grand Kahn from other electrical contractors?
  4. What kind of jobs does Grand Kahn get called for?
  5. What do people misunderstand about this trade?
  6. How do you know when a job is really finished?
D · Culture — Evergreen (GK Brand)
The Standard
  1. What are you proudest of about this crew?
  2. What's the standard at Grand Kahn?
  3. Why does this trade matter?
E · Close — Quote Gold
The Close
  1. If someone drives past this site five years from now, what's still here because of your work?
  2. What's the best part of this job for you?
