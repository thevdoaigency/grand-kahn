01The Market

A mature, fragmented market — with two genuinely hot growth lanes.

The US electrical contractors market is large ($237.6B in 2023, projected to $256.6B by 2029) but slow-growing and brutally fragmented — over 239,000 businesses nationally, thin margins, and a worsening skilled-labor shortage driving wage inflation across the trade. Flat growth and fragmentation is the market Grand Kahn competes in every day on price and availability.

But two sub-sectors are growing much faster than the category average: EV charging infrastructure and data center / AI power demand, both requiring exactly the kind of specialized, code-current electrical expertise a 117-year union shop is built for. Grand Kahn already has a foothold — a dedicated EV charger quote tool live on the site — that most competitors don't.

02A Finding Worth Fixing First

The legacy story doesn't match itself.

This is a brand hygiene issue with real strategic weight. The homepage banner reads "117 Years of Experience." The About page's own history timeline states S.M. Kahn Electric — "Chicago's first registered electrical contractor" — was formed in 1928, which is 98 years ago, not 117. A 2022 page description says "115 years," implying a founding year of roughly 1907 — which lines up with what Mike Casto says on camera in the June 30 interview footage, but not with the site's own written timeline.

The fix: pick one founding year — 1907 (S.M. Kahn's actual formation per the spoken company history) or 1928 (whatever the timeline entry is actually meant to reference) — correct the "Our History" section to match, and use that single number everywhere: website, LinkedIn, video, pitch decks. Right now the company's single strongest asset — being older than almost anyone else in the room — is undercut by not being able to say by how much.
03The Competitive Landscape

Legacy and certification are rare. Scale is not.

Chicagoland has over 600 qualified electrical contractors in the Powering Chicago directory alone. Most compete on price and availability. Few can claim both a century-plus legacy and a woman-owned leadership story — and almost none can claim both while also holding landmark clients like Tesla, Rush, and Radio Flyer.

04Where Grand Kahn Stands

SWOT, grounded in what's actually true today.

Strengths

  • Genuine multi-generational legacy — Chicago's first registered electrical contractor
  • Woman-owned leadership (CEO Jo Beth Leopardo) — rare in a heavily union, male-dominated trade
  • Union affiliations (NECA, IBEW) plus real safety/technical certs (NFPA 70E, OSHA 30, Licensed PE)
  • Landmark client roster: Tesla's Melrose Park training facility, Rush cancer center, Radio Flyer's flagship, City Hope
  • Already positioned in EV charging with a dedicated quote tool — ahead of most local competitors

Weaknesses

  • Site's News section hasn't posted since November 2022 — over three years stale
  • Internal inconsistency on founding year undercuts the single strongest brand asset
  • Landmark projects (Tesla, Rush, Radio Flyer) aren't visibly presented as case studies
  • No visible formal WBE/MBE certification badge — "woman-owned" is a claim, not yet a credential

Opportunities

  • EV charging and data center power are the two fastest-growing lanes in the category — Grand Kahn already has a head start
  • Formal WBENC / City of Chicago MBE-WBE certification opens diverse-spend-mandated municipal and institutional bids
  • AWC portfolio cross-sell: Leopardo Efficiency's municipal relationships are a warm door into public-sector electrical work
  • A fixed, consistent legacy number sets up the "Chicago since [year]" line already being tested on camera

Threats

  • Persistent electrician shortage and wage inflation compressing margins industry-wide
  • 600+ qualified contractors competing in the Chicagoland market, many on price alone
  • Certified diversity-boutique competitors (e.g., City Lights, Ltd.) directly contesting the same niche
  • Utility-scale infrastructure players can out-resource Grand Kahn on the biggest data-center and fleet-electrification projects

06The Growth Trajectory

What Grand Kahn could grow into, once the legacy converts to trust.

The move isn't "more electrical jobs." It's the move from old-line subcontractor to trusted power, infrastructure, technology, and mission-critical execution partner — the same arc four real electrical contractors have already run, at very different scales. None of them started where they ended up.

The Growth Line
Grand Kahn grows from "historic Chicago electrical contractor" into a modern electrical infrastructure partner for complex buildings, critical systems, and institutional owners.
Large-Contractor Comp

Rosendin

Founded 1919 by an immigrant electrician — almost the exact same origin story as Grand Kahn, just a generation earlier. Rosendin converted a family business into an employee-owned company (ESOP completed in 2000) and is now the largest employee-owned electrical contractor in the US: 12,000+ employees, roughly $5.6B in revenue. Proof that a founder-family legacy story and a modern ownership structure aren't in tension — they can be the same growth story.

Founded 1919 · Now 12,000+ employees, ~$5.6B revenue
Advanced Aspiration

M.C. Dean — "Building Intelligence"

The more advanced version of where systems integration leads: M.C. Dean designs, builds, operates, and maintains cyber-physical solutions for mission-critical facilities, secure environments, and complex enterprise infrastructure. This is the language of a contractor that stopped describing itself by trade and started describing itself by outcome.

Positioning: "Building Intelligence" — systems integration for mission-critical facilities
Public-Company Reference

IES Holdings

A public-market proof point (Nasdaq: IESC) for how electrical contracting expands into broader infrastructure — data centers, residential housing, commercial and industrial. Ranked #6 nationally on EC&M's Top 50 Electrical Contractors list, with 10,000+ employees and a Commercial & Industrial segment that grew 16% in fiscal 2025, driven partly by education and healthcare — Grand Kahn's own core sectors.

Nasdaq: IESC · #6 on EC&M's 2024 Top 50 Electrical Contractors
Midwest-to-National Story

Faith Technologies (FTI)

The most directly relevant comp geographically — founded 1972 in Menasha, Wisconsin, a genuine Midwest electrical contractor that grew into a national leader in engineering, construction, manufacturing, and clean energy, with an estimated $5B in revenue. FTI proves the exact path available to Grand Kahn: start as a regional Midwest contractor, don't move, and still become a national name.

Founded 1972, Menasha, WI · Now a national engineering & clean energy leader

Rosendin and Faith Technologies both prove the same thing from different directions: a real, unglamorous regional legacy — a family name, a Midwest zip code — is not a ceiling. It's the foundation the largest players in this category are still standing on, decades later. Grand Kahn already has the foundation. It has 117 years of it.
